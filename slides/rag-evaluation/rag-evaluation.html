Why RAG eval is hard

RAG systems

When the final answer is bad, you must know which stage failed — wrong documents fetched, or right documents and the LLM still botched it?

RAG · EVALUATIONTWO STAGES03 / 13
02 · Retrieval metrics

Stage 1 — classic IR, deterministic & cheap

Recall@kDid the relevant chunk make the top-k? Usually matters most — no answer in context, generation is doomed.
Precision@kHow much of what we retrieved was actually relevant? Noise hurts the LLM.
MRR / NDCGRanking — is the best chunk near the top? LLMs weight earlier context more heavily.
MRR → one right answer, first hit only · NDCG → many relevant chunks, graded, whole list
RAG · EVALUATIONRETRIEVAL METRICS04 / 13
02 · Generation metrics

Stage 2 — fuzzy, LLM-specific, needs a judge

FaithfulnessDoes the answer stick to the retrieved context — or hallucinate?
Answer relevanceDoes it actually address the question that was asked?
CompletenessDid it use all the relevant retrieved info, or leave some out?
? discussion If recall@k is high but answers are still wrong — where's the bug? (generation, or chunking / context-ordering)

04 · Who decides if it's good?

How do you actually grade output?

Human evaluationgold standard

Most trustworthy — but doesn't scale, expensive, inconsistent between raters.

LLM-as-judgescales · biased

A strong model grades against a rubric. Scales well; prefers verbose / its own family, needs calibration.

Reference-basedmostly faded

Compare to a golden answer (BLEU / ROUGE / embedding sim). Weak for RAG — why these fell out of favor.

Golden sethalf the battle

Curated (question → relevant docs → ideal answer). Often synthetically generated, then human-reviewed.

05 · "What number tells me it's working?"

Metrics — there's no single number

You pick metrics per stage and per concern.

Retrieval
Recall@k · Precision@k · MRR · NDCG
Deterministic, cheap.
Generation
Faithfulness · answer relevance · completeness
Need an LLM or human judge.
System
Latency (p50/p95) · cost/query · tokens · throughput
Normal observability.
First two measure quality (need judgment); the third measures operations. RAG eval is the marriage of those two worlds.

05 · Don't conflate with online eval

Online monitoring — system health over time

Eval judges answer quality; monitoring watches the system — dashboards & alerts, plus RAG-specific signals.

Latency & costLLM calls are slow and expensive — a real SLA concern.
DriftQuestions shifting away from the knowledge base; retrieval scores trending down.
Failure signalsSpike in 'I don't know', low retrieval confidence, fallbacks firing.
Guardrail hitsToxicity, PII leaks, off-topic answers.
→ Datadog / Grafana / PagerDuty for your RAG pipeline

06 · A related axis — a whole separate session

Security & prompt injection

Quality eval is functional testing · security eval is adversarial testing — same loop, attack prompts instead of Q&A.

The RAG twist
Indirect prompt injection

A poisoned doc in your knowledge base says 'ignore previous instructions' — the retriever serves it into context. Like SQL injection, but the interpreter is an LLM.

The honest caveat
Mostly system design, not metrics

Authz before the vector search, guardrails, sandboxing. Eval measures whether defenses work — building them is architecture.
